We didn't start by guessing what flavours to combine. We started by asking what actually works. Eight ingredients examined against real health outcomes and real scientific literature — then formulated into combinations that make biological sense.

Our Process

Three Steps. No Shortcuts.

Every piece of produce that goes into a Glengala Fresh juice goes through the same cleaning process. It's not optional, and it's not for show.

1

Physical Removal

Produce is scrubbed to remove visible dirt, debris, and surface contamination before it touches anything else.

2

Ozone Bubbler Bath

Each item is submerged in an ozone-infused water bath. The O3 molecules penetrate and eliminate bacteria, fungi, parasites, and residual pesticides.

3

Final Ozone Rinse

A fresh ozone rinse removes anything the bath loosened. Within minutes, the ozone converts back to oxygen. No chemical residue. Nothing left behind.

Why Ozonated Water?

Most commercial produce is cleaned with chlorinated water. Chlorine leaves behind residue and is significantly less effective than ozone at killing pathogens.

Ozone (O3) is 3,100 times faster than chlorine as a disinfectant. It eliminates bacteria, viruses, fungi, algae, and parasites on contact. Then it simply reverts to oxygen.

It's classified by the US FDA as Generally Recognized As Safe. It's used in hospitals, food processing plants, and dentistry. And now it's part of how every Glengala Fresh juice starts.

The Worm Test

Sometimes we find worms in our celery. And honestly? That's a sign we've sourced something good. Worms can't survive on produce that's been treated with the level of pesticides most commercial farming uses. If worms are alive on it, the chemicals weren't harsh enough to kill them. That's the standard we source to.

Our Standards

Cleaner Than Organic
Isn't Just a Tagline.

Organic certification means a product meets a set of minimum standards. But certification is expensive, and plenty of produce grown without heavy chemicals never gets certified because the paperwork cost isn't worth it to smaller growers.

We don't rely on a label. We rely on ozonated water to eliminate whatever got through, and we source from suppliers whose produce survives the worm test.

It's a different standard. A real one.
