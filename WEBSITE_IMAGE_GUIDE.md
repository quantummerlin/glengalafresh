# 🎨 SPECIFIC SHOT LIST FOR YOUR BLACK-LIDDED PLASTIC BOTTLES

## Photography Tips for Your Specific Bottles

### Lighting
- **Natural light** works best for juice photography
- Use a softbox or window light to avoid harsh shadows on the plastic
- Backlight bottles slightly to show the juice color through the plastic
- The **black lid** will photograph cleanly against most backgrounds

### Background Recommendations
1. **White/light backgrounds** - Makes the colors POP (especially Beetroid red, Golden Fire orange)
2. **Wooden/rustic** - Farm-fresh, natural feel
3. **Marble** - Premium, luxurious
4. **Green foliage** - Natural, health-focused
5. **Dark/moody** - For "bold" products like Immuni-Fire

### Color Psychology Per Product
- **Beetroid** (deep red/purple): White or light wood backgrounds to pop the color
- **Immuni-Tea** (golden/yellow): Soft pastels, light blues work well
- **Immuni-Fire** (golden with heat): Reds, warm woods, dark backgrounds
- **Golden Fire** (orange/gold): Teal or dark contrasting backgrounds
- **Celery** (green): Natural wood, stone, or white

### Must-Have Shots Featuring the Black Lid
- Close-up macro of the sealed black lid (communicates "sealed fresh")
- Overhead shot looking down on bottles (black circles in pattern)
- 45-degree angle showing both lid and label

# 💡 PHOTOGRAPHY APPROACHES

## Option 1: Professional Photographer (Recommended)
- **Cost:** £500-2,000 for a full day
- **Gets you:** 30-50 high-quality images in one session
- **Best for:** Launch, hero images, key product shots

## Option 2: DIY Smartphone Photography
- **Tools needed:**
  - iPhone 12+ or Samsung Galaxy S21+ (modern phones)
  - Tripod (£20-30)
  - Softbox lights (£50-100 for a pair)
  - White foam board (£5) for bouncing light
  - Various backgrounds (wooden board, marble contact paper, etc.)
- **Apps:** Lightroom Mobile, Snapseed (for editing)
- **Cost:** £100-200 total for gear

## Option 3: Hybrid Approach (BEST VALUE)
1. Hire a photographer for 1 day to get 30 hero images
2. DIY the rest (lifestyle, social media, blog content)
3. Supplement with stock photos for backgrounds/icons
