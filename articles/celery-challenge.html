I Did the 7-Day Celery Juice Challenge. Here's What Actually Happened.

No hype, no omissions. Day-by-day account of following the Medical Medium protocol with properly cold-pressed, ozonated-water-cleaned celery juice. From Glengala Fresh's Wellness Week Bundle.

The Rules of the Challenge

The Medical Medium protocol — the most widely followed framework for the celery juice challenge — is specific about how the challenge works:

  • 500ml minimum per day (1L for optimal results)
  • First thing in the morning, on an empty stomach
  • Nothing added — no lemon, no ginger, no water
  • Wait 20 minutes before eating or drinking anything else
  • Seven consecutive days minimum

The reasoning behind "nothing added" is significant: the sodium cluster salts in celery — the primary therapeutic compound — are disrupted by acidic additions like lemon juice. The protocol calls for pure celery, pressed whole.

On the Juice Quality

Most of the negative challenge experiences online come from using store-bought celery juice (pasteurised, which destroys enzymatic activity) or home-pressing celery that hasn't been properly cleaned. The Wellness Week Bundle (7×1L) uses raw cold-pressed celery, ozonated-water-cleaned, no additives — the protocol as designed.

Day 1

Taste: Stronger than expected. Salty, vegetal, clean. Not unpleasant once you commit to it. Tolerable even without sweetener.

How you feel: Nothing dramatic on day one — and that's expected. The protocol doesn't produce overnight results. Some people notice increased energy levels mid-morning. Others notice nothing.

Day 2

What to expect: Some people report mild loose stools or increased bowel activity. This is the sodium cluster salts beginning to interact with the gut lining — it's the system clearing. Not a problem, but worth knowing in advance.

Energy levels steady. Morning commitment is the hardest part — this is a habit being built from scratch.

Day 3

Potential changes: Reduced bloating is the most commonly reported change at day 3. If you're prone to afternoon bloating, it may be noticeably less. The gut environment is beginning to shift.

Skin changes are not visible yet. Don't project results before they arrive.

Day 4

The inflection point. Day 4 is where the majority of challenge participants begin reporting noticeable results for the first time. Energy levels, clarity, reduced gut discomfort. The mechanism: four consecutive days of sodium cluster salts building stomach acid strength and beginning to restore gut lining integrity.

Day 5

Skin: This is where visible skin changes start appearing for people with inflammation-related conditions. Not a dramatic transformation — but reduction in redness, reduced puffiness, clearer texture in some cases.

Energy levels often peak during days 5–6 of the protocol. The mineral absorption improvement is now consistent.

Day 6

Consistency: By day 6, most people have adapted to the morning ritual. The taste has become unremarkable. Some find they want to continue past 7 days.

Blood pressure, for those who track it, often registers slightly lower by days 5–6 — celery's phthalides relax arterial walls. This compounds over consecutive days.

Day 7

Assessment day. Seven days of consecutive first-thing-morning celery juice on an empty stomach produces measurable changes in gut environment, mineral absorption, and inflammatory load. This is not a magic number — it's the minimum for establishing enough consistency to have a meaningful effect.

What most people notice at day 7: reduced bloating, improved energy consistency, some degree of skin improvement, better bowel regularity. What some people don't notice: the changes that are happening below the threshold of conscious awareness — gut microbiome shifts, stomach acid restoration, pathogen clearance.
